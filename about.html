The Challenge We're Solving

Understanding the critical gaps in current internship systems

NEP 2020 & Semester 5 Internships

The New Education Policy (NEP) 2020 emphasizes experiential learning through internships, particularly in Semester 5 of the 4-year undergraduate program. However, the current system faces significant challenges:

Experiential Learning Focus

NEP promotes hands-on learning through internships, fieldwork, and apprenticeships for practical skill development.

Strategic Timing

Semester 5 is optimal for internships after students complete foundational courses and are ready for practical exposure.

Credit-Based Evaluation

Internships are credit-bearing and require comprehensive reports reflecting learning outcomes.

Current System Failures

Lack of Industry Tie-ups

Limited partnerships with companies, absence of proper MoUs, and inadequate placement cells.

Poor Planning

Last-minute program implementation without proper planning or preparation.

No Mentorship

Absence of faculty supervision, monitoring, or structured follow-up mechanisms.

Geographic Limitations

Limited quality internship opportunities in rural and remote areas.

Administrative Failures

Poor coordination, inadequate funding, and unclear implementation guidelines.

Fake Internships

Students resorting to certificate mills and non-functional internship programs.
